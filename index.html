We are building an open-source archive of civilian-police interactions.

Be the one to make a positive change

It is our hope that this information can serve as a resource to community organizers looking to combat police brutality, to police departments seeking additional insights into the implementation of their training, and to researchers seeking to understand the situational dynamics that result in negative encounters with police.

With our smart team of engineers, users have access to information to better serve their community by combatting against police brutality. Police departments also have insightful information on implementation of their training.

According to a research article conducted by Frank Edwards, Hedwig Lee, Michael Esposito "Police violence is a leading cause of death for young men in the United States. Over the life course, about 1 in every 1,000 black men can expect to be killed by police."
